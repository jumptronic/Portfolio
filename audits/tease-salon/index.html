TEASE Salon
Website Audit

The site is in good shape. TEASE has a strong local reputation, clear pricing, and a booking flow that works — and the site reflects that. What follows are tune-up items, not a rebuild. The technical findings are mostly small fixes to hand to whoever manages your Squarespace. The content suggestions are about leading with identity — using what's already there more intentionally.

These items are for whoever manages your Squarespace account. None require a redesign — most can be knocked out in a single session. Share this page with them and they'll know what to do.

Needs attention

Fix it

No primary headline

The homepage has no H1 — the heading search engines use to understand a page. Your logo fills that role visually, but it's an image, not text.

Add a text headline to the hero. It can be visually styled — doesn't have to look like a plain heading.

Fix it

Missing meta description

No custom description is set for any page. Google auto-generates one — usually awkward and unconvincing.

Write a 1–2 sentence description per page in Squarespace's SEO settings. Think of it as a mini ad.

Fix it

Screenshot images in production

Several images are macOS screenshots — larger files, slower to load, and harder for search engines to index.

Re-export as JPG or WebP with descriptive filenames. "tease-salon-interior.jpg" not "Screen Shot 2025-10-01."

Fix it

Images missing alt text

Most photos have no description attached — invisible to screen readers and Google image search.

Add a brief description to each content photo in Squarespace's image settings.

Fix it

Filename captions on services page

A gallery near the bottom of the services page shows raw filenames as captions: "1.png", "2.png", etc.

Remove the captions or replace with meaningful labels.

Fix it

No social sharing preview

When someone shares the site via text or social, no image or description appears — just a blank or auto-generated preview.

Set a social sharing image (1200×630px) and short description in Squarespace's social settings.

Fix it

Schedule button doesn't link directly to booking

The Schedule CTA in the nav opens the booking platform, but the direct link isn't wired to the booking page itself — adding an extra step for the client.

Update the Schedule link to point directly to: teasesalon.mysalononline.com/Booking/

Keep an eye on

Watch it

Homepage title could be stronger

Currently reads "TEASE Salon" — accurate, but misses a chance to tell Google what you do and where.

Try: "TEASE Salon | Hair Color & Cuts in Saint Paul, MN"

Watch it

Cancellation policy as first impression

Necessary and fair — but it's one of the first things a new visitor reads. Can feel like rules before a welcome.

Consider a brief, warm summary on the homepage; move the full policy to the booking flow.

Watch it

Logo loads twice

The logo appears twice in the HTML — once for desktop nav, once for mobile. Both have correct alt text. Low priority, worth knowing.

Watch it

Inconsistent page title format

Homepage uses "TEASE Salon" while interior pages use "Page Name — TEASE Salon." Small inconsistency, easy fix.

Standardize to one format across all pages in Squarespace's SEO settings.

Watch it

Image load performance (unconfirmed)

Worth verifying that the video backgrounds aren't slowing things down on mobile.

Run a free check at pagespeed.web.dev and share results if you'd like a read on them.

The opportunity

Lead with identity.
Then explain the services.

The bottom of your homepage has one of the strongest frames on the site — logo centered over full-bleed moving hair. That composition should open the page, not close it. Below are suggested rewrites for each homepage section, starting with a hero that leads with who TEASE is, not what it offers.

Cancellation policy section

Current

STRICT AND ENFORCED 24-HOUR CANCELLATION POLICY! Please understand that when you forget or cancel your appointment without giving enough notice, we miss the opportunity to fill that appointment time…

Suggested

A note on appointments.

Your time — and ours — matters. We ask for at least 24 hours' notice on any cancellation or reschedule. A card is required to book, and late cancellations may incur a fee. Full details are available when you schedule.

Consider moving the full policy detail to the booking flow — it's the right place for it, and it keeps the homepage feeling like a welcome rather than a waiver.
